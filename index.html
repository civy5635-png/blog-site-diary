Hello, there!

I will be slowly building my blog site on a branch, so this is all I have for now. I really wish I'd known about external style sheets before I started my portfolio redesign; I'd have saved myself hours of aggravation. That's what I get for starting side projects before finishing my web-design class.

I spent so much time being trepidatious about getting to the HTML part of the class, and it's turning out to be my absolute favorite part. I'm coming to this late in life. I worked retail for 30 years because I never figured out what I wanted to be when I grew up. When computers became more prevalent in the 90s, I wish I'd looked into this then, when I had a younger, more functional brain. I just kind of assumed that coding was for people much smarter than me at the time. I could have had a much more lucrative life. Oh well, I've probably done more discovery and learning in the past two years than I did in the 30 years prior. I suppose that's the way life is supposed to work. You trade a young, healthy body, with a brain that spent far too much time stressing about stupid crap that doesn't matter, but in exchange, you get your priorities in order, and far better emotional intelligence-if you're lucky. I just wish it didn't come with a falling-apart body. Still, I wouldn't go back for anything.
